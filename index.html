My Entrepreneurial Journey

This personal website documents my entrepreneurial journey as part of the ENT 110 course. It highlights the problem I identified, the solution I developed, and the process of building and testing a prototype. This website serves as an ongoing academic portfolio of my work.

Problem Identification

Low health literacy causes many patients aged 18–65 attending urban community clinics to incorrectly follow newly prescribed medication instructions, especially within the first 60 days of prescription.

Complex, text-heavy, and culturally non-specific patient education materials make it difficult for patients to understand medication usage, dosage, and side effects. This leads to medication errors, avoidable complications, poor health outcomes, and preventable hospital readmissions.

Proposed Solution

The proposed solution is a patient-centered medication education platform designed to improve understanding and adherence. It uses simple language, visual icons, short explainer videos, and optional audio narration to communicate medication instructions clearly.

Patients access the information through QR codes provided at clinics or on mobile devices, making the solution accessible, culturally adaptable, and easy to use for individuals with varying literacy levels.

Prototype Development

As part of the venture creation process, I developed a low-fidelity prototype to demonstrate how the solution would work in practice. The prototype focuses on usability, clarity, and accessibility rather than full technical development.

It includes visual medication instructions, simplified dosage guidance, and multimedia explanations. User feedback was considered to identify areas for improvement and future iteration.

Entrepreneurial Journey & Reflection

This project documents my journey through the venture creation process, including problem identification, root cause analysis, research, ideation, solution design, lean canvas development, prototyping, and reflection.

Through this experience, I learned the importance of empathy, research-driven decision-making, and iterative design in entrepreneurship. The project strengthened my ability to identify real-world problems and develop practical, user-centered solutions.
